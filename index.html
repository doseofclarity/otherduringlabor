Abnormal Labor (Dystocia)

Dystocia = difficult or abnormal labor. Classified as protraction (slower than normal) or arrest (complete cessation) of cervical dilation or fetal descent[reference:0].

⏳ Protraction Disorders
• Latent phase: nullipara >20 hr, multipara >14 hr[reference:1]
• Active phase: <1 cm/hr nullipara, <1.5 cm/hr multipara[reference:2]
• Second stage: >1 hr no descent
⛔ Arrest Disorders
• No cervical change for ≥4 hr with adequate contractions[reference:3]
• Second stage arrest: nullipara >3 hr, multipara >2 hr (without epidural)
• Third stage: retained placenta >30 min[reference:4]
Causes: "3 P's" – Power (inadequate contractions), Passenger (fetal malposition, macrosomia), Passage (CPD, non-gynecoid pelvis)[reference:5].

Obstetrical Emergencies

Emergencies can occur without warning. The nurse must recognize risk factors and act immediately[reference:6].

🫂 Shoulder Dystocia
• Impaction of anterior shoulder behind pubic symphysis (<3% births)[reference:7]
• Risks: macrosomia, diabetes, obesity, multiparity[reference:8]
• Nursing actions: McRoberts maneuver, suprapubic pressure, hands‑and‑knees position; prepare for neonatal resuscitation[reference:9].
🎗️ Prolapsed Umbilical Cord
• Cord lies below presenting part → compression → fetal hypoxia[reference:10]
• Actions: knee‑chest/Trendelenburg, manually elevate presenting part off cord, cover cord with moist sterile saline, prepare for immediate cesarean.
💔 Uterine Rupture
• Complete tearing of uterine wall (≥1% after classical cesarean scar)[reference:11]
• Signs: sudden cessation of contractions, fetal bradycardia, maternal shock; emergency laparotomy.
🌊 Amniotic Fluid Embolism (AFE)
• Amniotic fluid enters maternal circulation → anaphylactoid reaction[reference:12]
• Signs: sudden dyspnea, cyanosis, hypotension, DIC; immediate CPR and supportive care.
🩸 Postpartum Hemorrhage (PPH)
• Blood loss >500 mL vaginal, >1000 mL cesarean. Causes: Tone (atony), Tissue (retained), Trauma (lacerations), Thrombin (coagulopathy)[reference:13].
• Actions: fundal massage, oxytocin, bimanual compression, prepare for blood transfusion.
🦷 HELLP Syndrome / Eclampsia
• Seizures in preeclamptic patient[reference:14].
• Actions: left lateral position, airway protection, magnesium sulfate, prevent injury, prepare for delivery.

Intrapartum Procedures & Interventions

💧 Amniotomy (AROM)
Artificial rupture of membranes to induce or augment labor. Risks: cord prolapse, infection. Assess FHR before and after; note color/odor of fluid[reference:15].
💧 Amnioinfusion
Infusion of warmed saline into uterus to relieve cord compression (variable decelerations) or dilute meconium. Monitor for uterine overdistension[reference:16].
🍼 Induction of Labor
Stimulation of contractions before spontaneous onset. Methods: oxytocin, prostaglandins (PGE2), mechanical (cervical ripening balloon). Bishop score ≥6 favorable[reference:17].
⚡ Augmentation of Labor
Strengthening already established but inadequate contractions (hypotonic). Administer oxytocin per protocol; monitor for hyperstimulation[reference:18].
🪖 Vacuum-Assisted Delivery
Soft cup applied to fetal head for traction; second stage prolonged. Nursing: assist positioning, document cup placement, monitor for caput/cephalohematoma.
🦯 Forceps-Assisted Delivery
Metal instruments to rotate or extract fetus. Higher risk of perineal lacerations and facial nerve injury; prepare for repair and neonatal assessment.
✂️ Episiotomy & Perineal Repair
Surgical incision to enlarge vaginal outlet (midline or mediolateral). Nursing: ice packs, perineal care, pain management, monitor for hematoma/infection.
🤰 Cesarean Birth (C‑section)
Surgical delivery through abdominal/uterine incisions. Indications: CPD, malpresentation, fetal distress, prior classical incision. Nursing: pre‑op preparation, post‑op fundal checks, pain control, VTE prophylaxis.

Fetal Monitoring & Distress

Fetal distress is most often diagnosed by abnormal FHR patterns. Three‑tiered system (Category I, II, III) guides management[reference:19].

CategoryDefinitionAction
INormal tracing: baseline 110–160, moderate variability, no late/variable decels, accelerations present or absentRoutine monitoring; reassure
IIIndeterminate: bradycardia not meeting criteria, minimal variability, variable decelerations without specific characteristics, tachycardiaInterventions (reposition, IV fluids, O₂, reduce contractions)
IIIAbsent baseline variability with recurrent late/variable decels, bradycardia, or sinusoidal pattern. Indicates fetal acidemia.Notify provider, prepare for immediate delivery (within 30 min)[reference:20]
VEAL CHOP mnemonic: Variable decels → Cord compression; Early decels → Head compression; Accelerations → Okay; Late decels → Placental insufficiency.

Newborn Resuscitation & Immediate Care

Approximately 10% of newborns require some assistance, 1% need extensive resuscitation. The NRP (Neonatal Resuscitation Program) algorithm is standard.

🔄 Steps of Resuscitation (Golden Minute)
1. Warmth, dry, stimulate; 2. Position airway, suction if needed; 3. Apneic/gasping → positive‑pressure ventilation (PPV); 4. HR <60 → chest compressions + PPV; 5. Epinephrine if HR remains <60 after 60 sec of compressions.
📏 APGAR Scoring
Assessed at 1 and 5 minutes; if low, repeat every 5 min. Score <4 = severe depression; 4–6 = moderate; 7–10 = good adaptation.
❄️ Thermoregulation
Prevent hypothermia: dry immediately, radiant warmer, skin‑to‑skin contact, warm blankets. Cold stress increases oxygen consumption and metabolic acidosis.
💊 Prophylaxis
Vitamin K IM (hemorrhagic disease prevention), erythromycin ointment (ophthalmia neonatorum), Hepatitis B vaccine within 12 hours.
Key point: The "Golden Minute" — first 60 seconds to evaluate and initiate resuscitation if needed. Initial steps (warm, dry, stimulate) are critical for spontaneous breathing.

Mnemonics & Key Pearls

🔁 VEAL CHOP (FHR patterns)
Variable = Cord compression; Early = Head compression; Accelerations = Okay; Late = Placental insufficiency
🩸 PPH causes (4 T's)
Tone (atony), Tissue (retained), Trauma (lacerations), Thrombin (coagulopathy)
🆘 Shoulder Dystocia maneuvers (HELPERR)
H – help, E – evaluate for episiotomy, L – legs (McRoberts), P – suprapubic pressure, E – enter (internal rotation), R – remove posterior arm, R – roll (all‑fours)
📏 Bishop Score (pre‑induction)
Dilation, Effacement, Station, Consistency, Position. Score ≥6 favorable, <6 unfavorable (cervical ripening needed)
💊 NRP Resuscitation (MR. SOPA)
Mask adjustment, Reposition airway, Suction, Open mouth, Pressure increase, Airway alternative
⚠️ TORCH + (infections causing hydrops)
Toxoplasmosis, Other (syphilis, HIV), Rubella, CMV, Herpes, Parvovirus B19
